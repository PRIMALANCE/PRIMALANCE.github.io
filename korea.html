COUNTRIES I WANT TO VISIT

KOREA

KOREA

Korea is special to me because it blends deep-rooted traditions with cutting-edge modernity in a way that captivates me. Its rich culture, from ancient palaces and hanbok traditions to K-pop and K-dramas, makes it an exciting place to explore. I’ve enjoyed K-dramas like Itaewon Class and Hospital Playlist, and visiting locations like Hongdae or the K-Star Road in Gangnam would be a dream come true. The food is another highlight—whether it's authentic Korean barbecue, spicy tteokbokki, or comforting bowls of kimchi jjigae, I’d love to experience Korean cuisine in its homeland. And of course, Korea’s tech scene, from futuristic cafés to AI-driven services, is something that amazes me.

To get there, I’d take a direct flight from Manila to Incheon International Airport, which usually takes around four hours. Once in Korea, the subway system and KTX (high-speed train) would make traveling between cities smooth and efficient.

While in Korea, besides exploring K-drama and K-pop hotspots, I’d love to visit Seoul for its vibrant city life, Busan for its beautiful beaches and fresh seafood, and Jeju Island for its breathtaking landscapes. Seoul itself offers so much—from the bustling streets of Myeongdong to the historic palaces of Gyeongbokgung and the futuristic attractions of Dongdaemun. Experiencing Korea firsthand would be an unforgettable adventure.
